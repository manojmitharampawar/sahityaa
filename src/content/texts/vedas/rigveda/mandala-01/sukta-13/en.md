### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 13
**Rishi**: Medhatithi Kanva | **Deity**: Various (Apri) | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
O well-kindled Agni, bring the gods hither for the worshiper who brings oblations. O Purifier, O Hotar, sacrifice to them. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
O Tanunapat (son of the self), O sage Agni, make our honey-sweet sacrifice to the gods today for their feasting. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
I invoke hither the beloved Narashansa (praised by men), the sweet-tongued performer of sacrifice, to this our offering. || 3 ||

## Verse 4
O Agni, being invoked, bring the gods hither in thy most comfortable chariot. Thou art the Hotar established for men. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
Spread out the sacred grass (barhis) in order, O wise ones, its surface smeared with butter, where the vision of immortality is seen. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
May the divine doors, the multipliers of truth, open wide; may they be irresistible, so that the sacrifice may surely be performed today. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
I invoke Night and Dawn, the well-adorned ones, to this sacrifice. May they sit down upon this our sacred grass. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
I invoke those two divine Hotars (priests), the sweet-tongued sages (kavis). May they perform this our sacrifice. || 8 ||

## Verse 9
Ila, Sarasvati, and Mahi, the three peace-giving and unfailing goddesses; may they sit upon the sacred grass. || 9 ||

## Verse 10
I call first upon Tvashtar, the foremost, the lord of many gifts. May that Maghavan (wealthy one) protect us in this sacrifice. || 10 ||

## Verse 11
O Lord of the Forest (Vanaspati), release the oblation to the gods, O Agni; dwell in brightness for the giver. || 11 ||

## Verse 12
Offer the sacrifice with 'Svaha' in the house of the worshiper for Indra. There I invoke the gods. || 12 ||
