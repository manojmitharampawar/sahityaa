### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 11
**Rishi**: Jetar Madhuchandasa | **Deity**: Indra | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
All hymns have magnified Indra, who is as vast as the ocean, the best of charioteers, the lord of spoils, the protector of the good, and the master. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
In thy friendship, O Indra, we warrior-people shall not fear, O lord of power. We laud thee, the victor and the unconquered. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
The chariot of Indra, which is new and full of power, is revealed. Now, grant us protection and carve out wealth for us. || 3 ||

## Verse 4
Thou, O wielder of the thunderbolt, didst open the cave of Vala who possessed the kine. The gods, when troubled by the foe, threw themselves upon thy protection. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
Through thy gifts, O hero, I have returned safe across the rivers. We recognize thy friendship, O all-seeing one, as our happiness. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
O wise one, open the paths for us that lead to welfare. May that most bountiful Indra give us sustenance that supports a thousand. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
By thy wiles (wisdom), O Indra, thou didst cast down the wily Shushna. The wise know thy glorious deeds; their praises go higher. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
O Indra, of many powers, thou art the master of wealth for our kinship. O Shakra (powerful one), give us the accumulated riches. || 8 ||
