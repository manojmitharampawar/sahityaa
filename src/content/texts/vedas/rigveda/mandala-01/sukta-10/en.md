### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 10
**Rishi**: Madhuchandas Vaishvamitra | **Deity**: Indra | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
The chanters of the Sama sing to thee, the singers of hymns laud thee with songs; the priests, O Shatakratu, raise thee aloft as a bamboo pole. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
When the worshiper climbs from ridge to ridge (of effort) to perform more work, then Indra knows his purpose and moves with his host of powerful ones. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
Yoke thy two haired bay horses, the powerful ones that fill the girth. Then, O Indra, drinker of the Soma, listen to our hymns. || 3 ||

## Verse 4
Come hither, join our songs, speak out and answer us. Grow our sacred knowledge (brahma) and increase our strength. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
Our praise is to be sung to Indra, the magnifier, the repeller of many foes; so that the Shakra may delight in our libations and in our friendship. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
Thee indeed we seek for friendship, for riches, and for heroic sons. May that Shakra Indra be able to grant us wealth. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
Easy to be opened, easy to be won, when the Maghavan (wealthy one) speaks; there is no one who can turn thee away. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
Indeed his truthful speech is vast and full of kine, like a ripe branch for the one who gives. || 8 ||

## Verse 9
Though all these people call thee in various ways for their protection, mayest thou be ours alone. || 9 ||

## Verse 10
By these unswerving hymns of praise, our path is made easy. O Indra, grant us might for our victory. || 10 ||

## Verse 11
May these powerful Somas delight thee, O Shatakratu; may they make thee strong in battles. || 11 ||

## Verse 12
Having drunk of this, O Hundred-powered, become the slayer of Vritra; protect the warrior in battles. || 12 ||
