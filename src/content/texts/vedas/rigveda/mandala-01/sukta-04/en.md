### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 4
**Rishi**: Madhuchandas Vaishvamitra | **Deity**: Indra | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
As the milker calls the good milch-cow for his aid, so we call every day the performer of good deeds, the beautiful-bodied Indra, for our protection. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
Come to our libations, O drinker of the Soma; drink of the juice. The intoxication of the wealthy one is indeed a giver of kine. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
Then may we know thy nearest and best thoughts. Do not pass us by, but come hitherward to us. || 3 ||

## Verse 4
Go to the wise, the invincible Indra, and ask him of the sage, who grants the best gifts to thy friends. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
Let our revilers say what they will: let them depart elsewhere; let us persist in doing service to Indra alone. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
And may the people call us fortunate, O destroyer of foes, in thy friendship; may we remain in the protection of Indra. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
Bring this quick-flowing Soma for the swift Indra, the beauty of the sacrifice, the delighter of men, that gladdens the friend. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
Having drunk of this, O Hundred-powered (Shatakratu), become the slayer of Vritra; protect the warrior in battles. || 8 ||

## Verse 9
Thee, the warrior in battles, we urge on, O Shatakratu, for the winning of wealth and power. || 9 ||

## Verse 10
Sing ye to that Indra who is the great support of riches, the good ferrier across, the friend of the juice-presser. || 10 ||
