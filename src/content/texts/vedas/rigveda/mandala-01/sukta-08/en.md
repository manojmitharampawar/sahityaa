### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 8
**Rishi**: Madhuchandas Vaishvamitra | **Deity**: Indra | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
All hymns have magnified Indra, who is as vast as the ocean, the best of charioteers, the lord of spoils, and the true master. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
In thy friendship, O Indra, we warrior-people shall not fear, O lord of power. We laud thee, the victor and the unconquered. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
The ancient gifts of Indra and his protections never fail, when he bestows upon his singers wealth of kine and sweet food. || 3 ||

## Verse 4
The breaker of cities, young, wise, and of infinite power, was born; Indra, the supporter of every work, the wielder of the thunderbolt, praised by many. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
Thou, O wielder of the thunderbolt, didst open the cave of Vala who possessed the kine. The gods, when troubled by the foe, threw themselves upon thy protection. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
Through thy gifts, O hero, I have returned safe across the rivers. We recognize thy friendship, O all-seeing one, as our happiness. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
We, being for that purpose alone, sing to thee, O Indra; to thee the sacrifices are lighted, O true lord, and with hymns we seek riches. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
May the wealthy and fortunate Indra know our praise well, and take the singer into his protection. || 8 ||

## Verse 9
Praise him, the powerful, the great, the very bountiful Indra; seek his might for our winning (of victory). || 9 ||

## Verse 10
O powerful and ever-giving Indra, open for us this wealth; thou whom no one can resist. || 10 ||
