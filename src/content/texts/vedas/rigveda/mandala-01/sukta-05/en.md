### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 5
**Rishi**: Madhuchandas Vaishvamitra | **Deity**: Indra | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
Come hither, sit down; sing forth to Indra, O friends, ye who bring hymns of praise. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
To the lord of many, the master of treasures, Indra, when the Soma is pressed. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
May he be with us in our work, in our wealth, in our thought; may he come to us with spoils of war. || 3 ||

## Verse 4
Whose bay horses in the fray the enemies cannot restrain, to that Indra sing ye forth. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
To the drinker of the juice, these pressed, pure Soma drops, mixed with curd, are going for his enjoyment. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
Thou, O wise Indra, for the drinking of the juice, wast born to greatness and supremacy. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
May the Soma drops enter thee as rivers enter the sea; naught, O Indra, surpasses thee. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
Growing in might, O Indra, thou hast made thyself most glorious and famous, when thou rulest over the Somas. || 8 ||

## Verse 9
In the rapture of this Soma, Indra subdues all the hosts of men, and is most glorious among the gods. || 9 ||

## Verse 10
We know thee, Indra, as the winner of wealth and the warrior in battles; come then quickly for our protection. || 10 ||
