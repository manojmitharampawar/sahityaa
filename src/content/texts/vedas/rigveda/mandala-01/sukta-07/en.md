### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 7
**Rishi**: Madhuchandas Vaishvamitra | **Deity**: Indra | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
The singers of hymns sing to the great Indra; the chanters of praise laud him with their songs; and all voices join in his praise. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
Indra is the friend of his two bay horses, the beloved of the pious, and the golden-armed wielder of the thunderbolt. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
Indra, for the long sight (of all), caused the sun to ascend the sky, and with his rays dispersed the dark clouds. || 3 ||

## Verse 4
O Indra, help us in our battles and in our great conflicts, thou powerful one, with thy mighty protections. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
We call upon Indra in great wealth (war), and Indra in small; he is our ally, the wielder of the thunderbolt against the foes. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
O bountiful and ever-giving Indra, open for us that cloud of wealth; thou from whom no one can turn away. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
In every act of power, there are further praises of the thunderer Indra; I find no sufficient hymn for his glory. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
Like a bull among the herd, he moves the people by his might; he is the lord, the irresistible one. || 8 ||

## Verse 9
Indra alone rules over all men, over all riches, and over the five tribes of men. || 9 ||

## Verse 10
We call upon Indra from every side for the sake of the people; may he be ours alone (for protection). || 10 ||
