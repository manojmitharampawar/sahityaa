### Rigveda - Mandala 1 Sukta 12
**Rishi**: Medhatithi Kanva | **Deity**: Agni | **Metre**: Gayatri
</div>

<div class="divider"></div>

## Verse 1
We choose Agni as our messenger, the all-knowing Hotar (priest), the performer of this sacrifice with skill. || 1 ||

## Verse 2
Agni is kindled by Agni; he is the sage, the lord of the house, the youth, the carrier of oblations, whose mouth is the sacrificial ladle. || 2 ||

## Verse 3
Praise the sage Agni at the sacrifice, the one whose law is truth, the god who drives away disease (or enemies). || 3 ||

## Verse 4
O divine Agni, be thou the protector of that master of oblations who serves thee as his messenger. || 4 ||

## Verse 5
To him who with oblations worships Agni for the feasting of the gods, O Purifier (Pavaka), be gracious. || 5 ||

## Verse 6
O Purifier, O shining Agni, bring the gods hither to our sacrifice and to our oblation. || 6 ||

## Verse 7
O Purifier, O shining Agni, being praised by us, bestow upon us abundant wealth. || 7 ||

## Verse 8
O Purifying Agni, with thy radiant and delightful tongue (flames), bring the gods hither and sacrifice to them. || 8 ||

## Verse 9
O Purifier, O shining Agni, bring the gods hither to our sacrifice and to our oblation. || 9 ||

## Verse 10
Go, Agni, on thy messenger-errand as in ancient times, with thy purifying radiance; bring the gods hither for us. || 10 ||

## Verse 11
Since the singers with oblations worship thee, O Agni, come hither in truth. || 11 ||

## Verse 12
O Agni, born for the one who has prepared the sacred grass, bring the gods hither; thou art our venerable Hotar. || 12 ||
